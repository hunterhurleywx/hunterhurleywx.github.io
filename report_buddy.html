How to Report

⚠️ SAFETY FIRST: Never put yourself in danger to get a photo or report. Your safety is more important than any report.

📸 Taking Good Photos

  • Landscape orientation - Hold your phone horizontally for wider view
  • Include reference objects - Trees, buildings, or poles help show scale
  • Clear view - Avoid shooting through windshield if possible (pull over safely)
💡 Hail Measurement Tips:
• Use a ruler or coin for size comparison in photo
• Measure the largest stones you find
• Report time hail started and stopped

📝 Submitting Your Report

  1. Select report type from dropdown menu at top
  2. For hail: Select size or describe if larger than 5"
  3. For damage: Briefly describe what you see
  4. Take photo using the capture button
  5. Swipe left to open tweet panel
  6. Copy tweet and post to X (Twitter)
  7. Download photo to attach to your tweet

📍 Location Accuracy

  • App automatically gets your GPS location
  • Swipe right to see detailed location info
  • Report where event is occurring, not where you are
  • If reporting something distant, estimate location in tweet
📞 When to Call Instead:
• Life-threatening situations - Call 911 first
• Large/violent tornadoes - Call NWS directly (see location panel)
• Multiple casualties or major damage

🌪️ What to Report

  • Tornado - Visible funnel cloud touching the ground with rotation
  • Funnel Cloud - Rotating funnel-shaped cloud NOT touching ground
  • Wall Cloud - Lowered cloud base with rotation under storm base
  • Hail - Report anything quarter-size (1") or larger
  • Wind Damage - Trees down, structural damage, power lines down
  • Flooding - Water over roadways, swift water rescues needed

🚫 What NOT to Report

  • Regular rain or non-severe thunderstorms
  • Lightning (unless damage occurs)
  • Small hail (less than 1" or quarter-size)
  • Gusty winds without damage
  • Dark clouds without rotation
